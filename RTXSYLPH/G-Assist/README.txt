In folklore and mythology:
Sylphs are elemental spirits of the air, capable of creating both gentle breezes and violent storms.

Originally described by Paracelsus in the 16th century, they are part of a system of elemental spirits that also includes gnomes (earth), salamanders (fire), and undines (water).
They are often depicted with large wings and may have sharp, angular faces.
As a descriptive term:
"Sylph" can simply mean a slender, graceful woman or girl, similar to how the mythological creatures were depicted in romantic eras.
